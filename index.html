about me

My journey into tech started with computer programming in college. As I learned Cobol, Pascal, JCL and Assembler, I was fascinated with how easily I could move from the big picture into the details of the program. As mainframe applications became less frequently used, I moved into network support, information security, and finally electronic health record.

But nothing was as exciting as building a program. I began to learn how to build websites to fit my own specific needs. After my first website was done, I realized I found something that gave me the versatility and creativity I was looking for in my work. By moving to front-end development, I can use my organization skills and eye for detail to partner with businesses to create and launch clean, functional websites.
